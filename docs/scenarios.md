# Worked Examples

Gally is a general capitalization engine, not a single product. These six scenarios — the situations
the protocol was designed around — show how the same mechanics serve very different real-world deals.
Each follows the pattern **Situation → Gally flow → Result**, with links to the concepts behind each
step. You can find live projects resembling these in the [Marketplace](/docs/explorer).

## 1. Community housing — escrow & rental yield

**Situation.** A local developer wants to build a multi-family residential building but can't get a
bank loan.

**Gally flow.** A validator vouches for the project's permits and locks coverage; the project opens for
[funding](/docs/concepts). The community contributes USDC and, once the goal is hit, receives
[deeds](/docs/concepts). Construction money is released [tranche by tranche](/docs/lifecycle) — land,
foundation, framing — each gated by a validator-approved proof.

**Result.** When tenants move in, rent is [deposited as revenue](/docs/economics); the investors' split
flows into the yield index, and holders claim their proportional monthly yield. A passive, real-estate-
backed income stream that retail could never access alone.

## 2. Heavy machinery & manufacturing — revenue share

**Situation.** A manufacturer needs a specialized CNC machine to fulfill a new contract but wants to
avoid predatory equipment leasing.

**Gally flow.** They tokenize the specific machine purchase. Retail investors fund it and hold deeds
tied to that equipment. Instead of rent, the project's policy specifies a **revenue share**: the
manufacturer pays a usage fee or a percentage of profits generated by the machine, with proof of usage
attested on Walrus.

**Result.** Holders earn a cut of the machine's productivity. The economics are identical to housing —
gross revenue in, three-way split, index updated — only the revenue *source* differs.

## 3. Inventory & trade finance — a term that closes at a target

**Situation.** A retail business lands a large order for imported electronics but lacks the cash to buy
the initial inventory.

**Gally flow.** The business tokenizes the purchase order as a **term-financing** project: it defines a
return target (principal plus margin). Investors fund the inventory and hold short-term deeds. As the
goods sell, sales revenue is deposited and distributed.

**Result.** This is programmable trade finance. Once cumulative investor distributions reach the return
target, **anyone can close the project** — investors have been made whole plus their margin, and
deeds are redeemed. A self-liquidating, fixed-horizon deal. See [closing in the lifecycle](/docs/lifecycle).

## 4. Business bootstrap — equity-equivalent dividends

**Situation.** Founders want to build a community co-working space and coffee shop, needing capital for
construction and the first months of operating expenses.

**Gally flow.** They launch a project where deeds represent a long-term stake in the business's net
revenue. Funds unlock sequentially — leasing, renovation, operations — through the tranche engine.

**Result.** As the business turns a profit, revenue sweeps into the pool and holders receive ongoing
dividends — effectively a decentralized equity stake with programmatic payouts, no cap table or
transfer agent required.

## 5. DeFi collateralization — composing your deed

**Situation.** You hold a deed in a profitable project and face a cash emergency, but you don't want to
sell your position.

**Gally flow.** Because a deed can be [wrapped](/docs/economics) into a plain, standard `Coin<T>`, it
becomes instantly usable across the Sui DeFi ecosystem. You wrap your deed and deposit the coin into a
lending protocol as collateral to borrow stablecoins.

**Result.** You keep your exposure and unlock liquidity. The trade-off is explicit: while wrapped, the
coin earns no Gally yield (the Diamond-Hand mechanism). When you're done, you unwrap back into a
yield-earning deed — with **zero** retroactive yield owed for the time it spent wrapped. This
composability is the whole point of the wrapping machine.

## 6. The facilitator default — accountability & slashing

**Situation.** A builder secures funding but mismanages it and misses a milestone deadline encoded in
the project.

**Gally flow.** The "Triangle of Repercussion" activates. Because the deadline passed with no approved
proof, **anyone** flags the default — no coordination needed. The protocol seizes the builder's
collateral and the undeployed escrow, and (if a related vouch was successfully disputed) the validator's
coverage, into a [compensation pool](/docs/economics).

**Result.** Wrapping is frozen and a grace window opens so every holder — including those whose deeds
were wrapped in DeFi — can unwrap. Then the pool is swept through the index and distributed pro-rata.
Investors are compensated from three layers in order: undeployed escrow → slashed coverage → entity
collateral. The project then closes. This is the worst case, and it is exactly the case Gally is built
to make survivable. Walk through the mechanics in [Trust & Security](/docs/security).
